2. Focus marketing on Asian-based soup flavours

Why?
The average spending per head per visit on non-Vietnamese cuisines lean heavily towards Asian cuisines. The data has been divided into 3 categories:

  • Asian (655 VND)
  • Western / European (419 VND)
  • Undetermined* (601 VND)
Asian-based soup flavours may be more likely to sell among the locals.

4. Introduce Take-Home Soup Packs product line

Why?
There is a significant preference for single-pack food packaging (61%) as compared to multi-pack packaging (39%) among Vietnamese locals.

Several brands in rural and urban Vietnam are instant packet brands, and they have a high penetration rate among locals. This means there is a demand for instant / quick meals, and the take-home packs can meet this demand.
Data source: Statista
Most Chosen FMCG Instant Noodle Brands in Vietnam:
  • Hao Hao: 60.9% (rural), 80.6% (urban)
  • 3 Mien: 63% (rural)
  • Kokomi: 58.1% (rural)
  • Gau Do: 53.6% (rural)
